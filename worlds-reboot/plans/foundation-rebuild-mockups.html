01 / Stackyard Golf

Point where
the ball goes.

The proposed control is direct and world-anchored: move a target on the turf, see a stable shot guide, then release. The camera finishes settling before aim begins and remains fixed under the finger.

Proposed gameplay frame · visual/control intent, not final artiPad landscape · touch-first
1World-space targetThe reticle lives on the turf. Camera angle and frame-rate changes cannot rotate the intended shot.
2Stable shot previewDeadzone and smoothing stop small finger wobble. The guide appears only after deliberate motion.
3Locked cameraAim activates only after the camera is ready. Position, look, sway, and FOV freeze for the gesture.
4Quiet HUDShot, hole, score, and one state cue. Hidden screens become inert, not merely transparent.
Aim enter→Aim ready→Aiming→Rolling→Settling→Next lieorHoled

Post-hole contract

  • One visible action: Next Hole.
  • No hold gesture and no invisible live controls.
  • The button is valid only in the Hole Card state.
  • Ball, score, camera, and gesture state enter the next hole together.
  • Play Again exists only on the final card.
02 / Gridlock Run

Read it.
Choose it. Move.

The proposed Gridlock is a close, three-lane action runner. Hazards are staged as readable decisions, the courier is the hero of the frame, and failure rewinds locally instead of replaying the first five seconds.

Proposed gameplay frame · visual/control intent, not final artCloser hero · authored hazard · local recovery
1Checkpoint before challengeLearning and progress are banked before a lethal combination. A miss never repeats the opening.
2Hero-scale courierArticulated silhouette, layered materials, readable equipment, and enough screen presence to carry the game.
3Readable hazard grammarWarning zone, edge light, action window, visible gap, and contrasting landing surface all tell the same story.
4Directional inputUp, down, left, and right trigger on movement threshold. No finger-up jump, hold delay, or dead timing band.
Race→Action buffered→Hazard→LandingorCrash→Local tether→Checkpoint
03 / Approval gates

Three choices
before code.

The foundation can support alternatives, but these decisions change the player contract and visual production. Approving them first prevents another technically complete game that feels wrong on the iPad.

Recommended

Golf: direct target

The guide follows where the player points in the world. It matches the expectation that the finger should point the ball toward the cup.

Choose A: direct reticle
Choose B: pull-back slingshot
Recommended

Gridlock: three lanes

Left/right choices add real agency; up/down handle jump and slide. The simpler alternative keeps the runner fixed in the centre.

Choose A: four-direction runner
Choose B: up/down rhythm runner
Recommended

Hero: athletic courier

A semi-human android silhouette with visor, fabric/shell layers, parcel rig, expressive motion, and distinctive rival colorways.

Choose A: courier/android
Choose B: fully robotic redesign
